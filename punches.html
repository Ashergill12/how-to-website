Different Punches

In boxing there are 6 main punches that coaches teach and have numbered.

An odd numbered punch will be with your left hand and even numbered with your right (swapped for left handed people).
  1. Jab: a quick punch that is for setting up range and combos. its not meant to for pain or a knockouts, its for quick points.

  2. Cross/Straight: a punch that is mostly used right after a jab to try to knockout your oppnent while keeping your range.

  3. Lead hook: A heavy hitter punch only used in close range that is used at the end of combos but you don't need to use it in a combo

  4. Hook: Same at the lead one but more powerful but slower

  5. Lead uppercut: a very close range punch used in combos to hit heavy or get through there gaurd

  6. Uppercut: powerful punch to knockout your oppnenet but slower then the lead uppercut

How to Punch

In boxing there is a main basic stance that everyone learns. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, with your lead foot (left foot for right handed) slightly forward. keep your knees slightly bent and your weight balanced between your feet. and raise your hands to protect your face, with your lead hand near your chin and your rear hand guarding your jaw.

When your in this stance the way to jab is extend your arm staright at your oppenent at there eye level. you fist should be rotated so your palm would be facing down.
you want to snap your jab and keep it quick, rather then powerful
and always take a small step forward at the same time as you punch. and after the punch you bring your hand right back so your oppnent has no chance to hit you




Cross

With a cross you want it to be powerful so you have to twist you body.
So before you punch you have to twist you back leg like your sqaushing a bug on the ground and then twist you hip at the same time. And at end of the rotation you want to throw your rear hand straight at you oppenets face not down and then bring it right back to your face.


Hook

As you prepare to throw the hook, you have pivot on your lead foot and rotate your hips and shoulders toward the target. This generates power from your lower body.
Bend your elbow at a 90-degree angle. Your fist should be clenched, and your palm should face you as you throw the punch.
Swing your arm in a circular motion toward the target, aiming for the side of your opponent's head or body.

Uppercut

Slightly drop your weight to load power into your legs. This coiling motion is important for generating force.
As you throw the punch, twist your hips and shoulders to drive the uppercut upward. The power should come from your core, not just your arm.
You have to punch upward with your palm facing towards you. Don't swing to hard becuase this loses accuracy and creates space for an oppnent to hit you.
